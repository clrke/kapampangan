🇵🇭 Kapampangan Cheat Sheet

Read & reply in Kapampangan without getting lost in the mix of Tagalog & English.

📖 Why it’s tricky

Texted Kapampangan is spelled phonetically and casually, and it’s freely mixed with Tagalog and English loanwords (fren, post, chat, pwede). The trick is to ignore the borrowed words and decode the short connective words — that’s where the real Kapampangan lives.

🧩 Patterns that unlock sentences

  • Pronoun clusters — ku (I), ke / ne (I…it / he-she…it), mi / tamu (we / our), ya (he/she). They get glued onto verbs.
  • Negation — E + pronoun → Eku (I don’t), Ela (they don’t).
  • Borrowed words stay English/Tagalog — pwede, post, fren, resort. Focus your decoding on the short connective words.

💬 Handy reply phrases

💡 Tip: when stuck, fall back to Tagalog — most Kapampangan speakers are fully bilingual, and mixing is completely normal in texts.
